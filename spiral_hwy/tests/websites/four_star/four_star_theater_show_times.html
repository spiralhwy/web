A People's History of North American Music

PG-13

A PEOPLE'S HISTORY OF NORTH AMERICAN MUSIC A new lecture / film / slideshow that attempts to tell the entire history of North American music in ninety minutes! Mississippi Record label and store founder Eric Isaacson will guide you through this whirlwind presentation of archival film, sound clips and images. Subjects include: - Examples of industrialization devolving the human race to become dumber while other animals are getting smarter. - The insane early American Puritan's visions of a utopian musicless society. - How The Church Of Scientology saved the record industry. - A brief history of Mississippi Records and details about the many chips on their shoulders. - The dystopian world of Spotify and AI music. - Hopeful messages about the future. Overall, it's the story of how the powers that be try to control music and people throughout history finding ways to work outside puritans, corporations and tech lords' systems despite these social engineers' best efforts. Don't worry, it's a fun telling of this tale with lots of great music, images and wild stories. About the presenter - Eric Isaacson founded the Mississippi Records store and label in 2003. The label has released over 360 records and 160 cassettes. Eric has toured with presentations like this one for the past eleven years, performing over two hundred shows in over forty different countries.

Dark Entries + CTC present: Panoramic Colorsound

DARK ENTRIES + THE CREATIVE TECHNOLOGY CONSORTIUM PRESENT: PANORAMIC COLORSOUND THIS EVENT WILL BE A VISUAL EXPLORATION CONCEPT OF TELEVISION & FILM FROM THE 70s, 80s, 90s, AND TODAY WITH THE MUSICAL ACCOMPANIMENT OF THE ALBUM With their 300th release, Dark Entries has continued their track record of exposing us to the obscure and wonderful.  Comprised of Cooper, Letkiewicz and Bisenius, The Creative Technology Consortium’s Panoramic Colorsound transports listeners through the depths of their own creativity with 25 original compositions.   Conjured as a modern cinematic listening experience, inspired by film and television of the 80’s, 90’s and today, the soundtrack is defined by rippling harmonic waves of guitar, bass, and electronic modulation only able to be cut through by impactful percussion.  The trio truly grab the attentive by the hand and take us on a journey throughout the album.   The introspective “Down The Hall – What We Make Happen” slows the mind with hypnotic arpeggios and guitar riffs filling the air between soft rhythmic percussion and dream-like voices / synths flying through the air before fading away, as they arrived.  The lullaby-esque guitar outro is soon snapped by the syncopated drum programming and distorted synthesis of “Far From Amateur.”  The only penetrable sounds through this fortress are conversely pure in timbre.  Later on “Sympathetic Ear” walks us inside a grid-like world of intrigue with the deep chugging bass supporting synthetic arpeggios before communicating with interweaving waves of electric guitar, rhythmic snares, and what might be laser beams from approaching ships.   Simply put CtC’s Parnoramic Colorsound stands alone as an album for a closed-eye visual experience, but evolving into a nearly 3hr film that encourages focused listening and visual exploration. Special intro and Q&A with a member of CtC to follow the film!
